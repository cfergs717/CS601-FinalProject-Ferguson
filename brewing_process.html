Notes about the video

This video does an excellent job of outlining the entire brewing process, but I thought it would be educational to point out some key differences between what's described in the video (traditional, German methods) and what occurs today in the U.S.

  • Most American breweries don't use Kieselguhr (Diatomaceous Earth). It's an old filtration method used by the Germans, and on top of its difficulty of use, it's carcinogenic to breathe in the powder (weirdly, it's totally fine to ingest/drink once in the beer)
  • LOTS of other adjuncts are used today in the U.S., such as fruits, nuts, herbs, etc... the Germans hate that we do this (LOL).
  • Most breweries are not large enough for the brewer to be sitting at a desk behind a computer screen. Brewers are basically glorified janitors.
  • Multi-step mashing (as described in the video) is more common in German brewing. Americans tend to use "Single Infusion" mashing, where you mash in at one temperature and let it rest.
  • I could name plenty more, but you're probably bored by now (:
